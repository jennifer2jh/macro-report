Energy
UAE Exits OPEC (effective May 1) — "Hardest blow ever." OPEC's survival in question
Morning Brew / Bloomberg ▾
The UAE announced it is leaving OPEC and OPEC+, effective May 1. As OPEC's third-largest producer (12%+ of supply), this is a historic blow to the cartel's ability to coordinate and control oil prices.
  • Why leave: OPEC quota caps UAE at 3.4M b/d vs actual capacity of 4.8M b/d. Goal: reach 5M b/d by 2027
  • Geographic edge: UAE can export ~50% of oil overland, reducing Hormuz dependency vs other members
  • Geopolitics: Regional rivalry with Saudi Arabia + retaliation against Iran (fellow OPEC member) drone attacks
  • Why now: During war-related production cuts, departure causes minimal incremental disruption
  • Short-term: no immediate price relief (UAE not adding supply yet). Long-term: oil price volatility increases structurally
💡 Big Picture

OPEC was already weakened by the US shale revolution. UAE's exit raises a genuine question: can OPEC survive as a meaningful cartel? If not, oil price volatility increases permanently — complicating inflation forecasts and every central bank's job.
